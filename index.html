Charles Babbage

Mathematician, philosopher, inventor and mechanical engineer, Babbage originated the concept of a digital programmable computer.

Babbage is considered by some to be "father of the computer".

Early Life


His birthplace is disputed but many believe he was born in London.

A blue plaque on the junction of Larcom Street and Walworth Road commemorates the event.

Education


He attended the University of Cambridge.

He was already self-taught in some parts of contemporary mathematics and was disappointed in the standard mathematical instruction available at the university.

Interesting fact...


Babbage is credited with inventing the first mechanical computer, the Difference Engine.
